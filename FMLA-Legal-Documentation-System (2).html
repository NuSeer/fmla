⚠️ HR Communication Best Practices

  • Always communicate in writing: Email creates a paper trail
  • CC your personal email: Forward all HR emails to personal account
  • Use certified mail for formal complaints: Get proof of delivery
  • Take notes during phone calls: Document date, time, who spoke, what was said
  • Request written responses: "Please confirm in writing"
  • Follow up in writing: "Per our phone conversation on [date]..."
  • Save everything: Emails, letters, receipts, everything
  • Never delete: Even if HR resolves the issue

📚 FMLA Law Reference Guide

🏛️ Federal Law: Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) - 29 USC § 2601 et seq.

📖 Basic Entitlements (29 USC § 2612)

  • 12 weeks (480 hours) of unpaid, job-protected leave per 12-month period
  • Available for: birth/adoption, serious health condition (yours or family member's), military family leave
  • Leave can be taken intermittently or on a reduced schedule when medically necessary
  • Employer must maintain your health insurance during leave
  • You must be restored to the same or equivalent position upon return

⚠️ Retaliation & Interference Prohibitions (29 USC § 2615)

  • It is UNLAWFUL for an employer to:
  • "Interfere with, restrain, or deny the exercise of" FMLA rights
  • "Discharge or discriminate against" anyone for using FMLA leave
  • Use FMLA leave as a negative factor in employment decisions
  • Count FMLA leave against attendance policies
  • Retaliate for filing a complaint or participating in FMLA proceedings

📋 Specific Regulatory Standards (29 CFR § 825)

29 CFR § 825.220(c) - Use of FMLA Leave
"An employer may not use the taking of FMLA leave as a negative factor in employment actions, such as hiring, promotions or disciplinary actions."
29 CFR § 825.220(d) - Restoration to Position
"If an employee is unable to perform an essential function of the position because of a physical or mental condition, including the continuation of a serious health condition, the employee has no right to restoration to another position under the FMLA."
29 CFR § 825.216(a) - Attendance Policies
"Employers cannot use an employee's use of FMLA leave as a negative factor in employment actions, such as in calculating whether the employee has exceeded the allotment of disciplinary action points under a 'no-fault' attendance policy."

⚖️ What Constitutes Retaliation?

  • Disciplinary actions taken immediately or shortly after FMLA use
  • Public questioning or humiliation about FMLA usage
  • Changes in treatment after requesting or using FMLA leave
  • Holding employees to different standards after FMLA use
  • Creating hostile work environment due to FMLA usage
  • Threats or intimidation about future FMLA use
  • Denying benefits or opportunities because of FMLA leave

💰 Legal Remedies Available (29 USC § 2617)

  • Wages, salary, employment benefits lost due to violation
  • Other actual monetary losses (out-of-pocket expenses)
  • Interest on amounts owed
  • Liquidated damages equal to actual damages (double damages)
  • Reinstatement or promotion
  • Attorney's fees and court costs
  • Injunctive relief (court orders to stop violations)

⏰ Statute of Limitations (29 USC § 2617(c))

  • 2 years from date of last violation (general)
  • 3 years if violation was willful
  • DO NOT WAIT - File as soon as possible while evidence is fresh

📞 How to File a Complaint

  • Department of Labor - Wage and Hour Division (WHD)
  • Online: https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/fmla/complaint
  • Phone: 1-866-4-USWAGE (1-866-487-9243)
  • In Person: Visit your local WHD office
  • Private Lawsuit: Contact an employment attorney
  • Find attorney: National Employment Lawyers Association

📝 What You Need to File

  • Your name and contact information
  • Employer's name and address
  • Dates of FMLA leave taken
  • Description of the violation(s)
  • Any supporting documentation (this dashboard provides all of this!)

🛡️ Protection from Retaliation for Filing

29 USC § 2615(a)(2) & (b)
It is unlawful for an employer to "discharge or in any other manner discriminate against any individual" for opposing unlawful FMLA practices or filing charges, testifying, or participating in FMLA proceedings.

Translation: They CANNOT retaliate against you for filing a DOL complaint or talking to a lawyer. If they do, that's a SEPARATE violation.
